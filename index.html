Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj

Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj (1630â€“1680) was a legendary Maratha warrior king who founded the Maratha Empire in western India. Born in a turbulent period of regional conflicts and foreign invasions, Shivaji emerged as a visionary leader known for his strategic brilliance, military acumen, and commitment to justice. He established a decentralized administrative system, fortified strategic forts, and championed cultural and religious tolerance. Shivaji's legacy lies in his unwavering resolve to establish a just and independent realm, inspiring generations with his leadership values and principles.

Biography

1630: Shivaji Bhonsle was born on February 19th in the Shivneri Fort near Pune, to Shahaji Bhonsle and Jijabai.

1645: Shivaji, at the age of 15, captured the Torna Fort, marking his first significant military achievement.

1646: He acquired the Rajgad Fort, establishing his base for future campaigns.

1659: After a period of conflict, Shivaji signed the Treaty of Purandar with the Mughal Empire, ceding a few forts and agreeing to serve as a Mughal vassal.

1660: Shivaji managed to escape from the Mughal captivity in Agra and returned to the Deccan.

1674: He was crowned as the Chhatrapati (King) of the Maratha Kingdom on June 6th at Raigad Fort.

1675: Shivaji conducted the successful raid on Surat, a prominent Mughal trading center.

1679: He launched a naval campaign to seize control of important coastal areas and established a strong naval presence.

1680: Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj passed away on April 3rd at the Raigad Fort. He was succeeded by his son, Sambhaji.
